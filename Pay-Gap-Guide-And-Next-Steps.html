What "Feminine-Coded" and "Masculine-Coded" Mean

These terms don't describe who is doing the work â€” they describe how society historically categorised different types of labour.

Feminine-coded roles are jobs or tasks that have traditionally been associated with people, including:

  • administration
  • coordination
  • people support
  • care work
  • communication roles
  • emotional labour
  • organisational glue work

These roles are often perceived as "helping," "assisting," or "supporting," even when they carry critical operational responsibilities.

Masculine-coded roles are jobs or tasks culturally associated with men, including:

  • operations
  • trades
  • technical roles
  • analysis
  • strategy
  • decision-making

These roles are framed as more authoritative, strategic, or essential â€” even when the actual work overlaps heavily with feminine-coded roles.
