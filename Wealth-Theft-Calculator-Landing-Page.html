EQUAL SKILL - UNEQUAL PAY

These aren't "different jobs." They're the same skills with different labels — and wildly different paychecks.

That's not a coincidence. It's systemic.

Same Educational Investment. Different Returns

Teachers (4-year degree + ongoing training) earn $60 – 80 K. Engineers (same degree requirement) earn $90 – 130 K. Both solve complex problems. Both shape outcomes.

Same Responsibility for Lives. Different Value

Nurses ($70 – 90 K) vs paramedics ($50 – 70 K). Both make split-second, life-or-death decisions. Both require years of training. One is majority-women. One is majority-men. Guess which pays more?

Same Expertise in Development. Different Recognition

Early-childhood teachers ($45 – 65 K) vs electricians ($65 – 90 K). Both require technical skill and years of training. Both are licensed professionals. Only one gets paid like it.

Same Complexity. Different Label

Social workers ($55 – 75 K) vs corporate coordinators ($65 – 85 K). Same stakeholder management, same crisis handling, same documentation. Different label. Different pay.

Same Tech Skills. Different Value Tag

Librarians ($60 – 75 K) vs IT support ($65 – 85 K). Each manages complex information systems; many librarians even hold postgraduate degrees.

People Management Pays Less Than Process Management

HR managers ($75 – 95 K) vs operations managers ($90 – 120 K). Same strategic planning, same stakeholder management, same organizational impact. Different label, different pay.
