Welcome: You're Here Because You're Done

If you're reading this, you've already made one of the hardest decisions of your career: you're leaving.

Maybe negotiation failed. Maybe you never even tried because you knew—you just knew—they'd never pay you fairly. Maybe you've watched less experienced men get promoted past you one too many times. Maybe the gaslighting finally broke something inside you that you're not willing to let them break anymore.

Whatever brought you here, I want you to know something: You're not abandoning your career. You're saving it.

I'm writing this from a caravan in Western Australia with $1.26 in my bank account. I'm 60 years old, and I've been working for 45 years. I lost an estimated $1.5-2 million to wage discrimination over my career. I managed teams, I supported $30 million operations, I held senior responsibilities—and I was systematically underpaid for all of it.

And I stayed too long.

I stayed through the abuse. I stayed through the HR dismissals. I stayed until I had a breakdown at 48 and lost my house. I stayed because I didn't know how to leave strategically, and by the time I left, I left in crisis.

That's not going to be you.

This blueprint exists because I wish someone had handed it to me 20 years ago and said: "Here's how you leave powerfully. Here's how you protect yourself. Here's how you land somewhere better."

That's what we're doing together now.

You're Not Abandoning Your Career—You're Saving It

Let me be direct: staying at a job that undervalues you isn't loyalty. It's slow-motion career suicide.

Every year you stay:

  • The pay gap widens (they give you 2%, the market moves 5%)
  • Your skills get pigeonholed into their outdated version of your role
  • Your confidence erodes from constant undervaluation
  • Your retirement savings suffer from years of lower super contributions
  • Your resume stays frozen at their deflated title
  • Your network stagnates within their limited world

You're not leaving because you're a quitter. You're leaving because you're a professional who deserves to be paid like one.

The women who leave strategically don't just recover their careers—they leapfrog ahead. I've watched women double their salaries by leaving. I've seen women finally get the titles that match what they've been doing for years. I've seen women discover that the "difficult personality" they were told they had was actually competence that threatened insecure managers.

Your current employer has told you—through their pay decisions, their promotion decisions, their dismissal of your worth—exactly how they see you. Believe them. And leave.

But leave strategically. Leave with evidence secured. Leave with a financial runway. Leave with a better offer in hand. Leave with your dignity intact and your bridges unburned.

That's what this blueprint will help you do.

Exit Readiness Quiz: Are You Ready Now or Do You Need Prep Time?

Before we dive into the six-month timeline, let's figure out where you are right now. This isn't about judgment—it's about clarity.

Answer YES or NO to each question:

FINANCIAL READINESS

  • Do you have at least $3,000 in accessible savings?
  • Could you cover your essential expenses for 3 months if needed?
  • Do you know your minimum acceptable salary for a new role?
  • Have you reviewed your current expenses and identified what you could cut if needed?
  • Do you understand your Centrelink options if there's a gap?

DOCUMENTATION READINESS

  • Do you have copies of your performance reviews (saved outside company systems)?
  • Have you saved examples of your best work to a personal device?
  • Do you have documentation of your actual job responsibilities?
  • Have you saved your pay stubs and employment contract?
  • If relevant, have you documented incidents of discrimination or harassment?

CAREER READINESS

  • Is your resume updated with your real achievements (not just tasks)?
  • Is your LinkedIn profile current and optimised?
  • Do you have professional references lined up?
  • Have you researched what roles like yours actually pay in the market?
  • Do you have a clear target for what type of role you want next?

EMOTIONAL READINESS

  • Have you genuinely accepted that this employer will never pay you fairly?
  • Are you prepared to job search while still employed (the stress of two jobs)?
  • Can you keep your job search confidential from your current employer?
  • Do you have emotional support systems in place?
  • Are you clear that leaving is about your future, not revenge on them?

SCORING

16-20 YES: You're ready to start the 6-month exit timeline now. You've got the foundation in place. Proceed to Chapter 2 and begin Week 1.

11-15 YES: You're close, but shore up the gaps first. Spend 2-4 weeks addressing your NO answers, then begin the timeline. Focus especially on financial runway and documentation—these are non-negotiable.

6-10 YES: You need 1-2 months of preparation before starting the strategic exit timeline. Don't rush this—leaving without preparation can leave you worse off. Use this guide to build your readiness, starting with financial buffer and evidence preservation.

0-5 YES: You're not ready to leave yet, but you're ready to prepare. Spend 3-6 months building your exit foundation while staying employed. This isn't failure—this is wisdom. Use the time to save money, document everything, and build your escape velocity.

REALITY CHECK: If you scored low but you're in an abusive or dangerous situation, your timeline is different. Your safety matters more than strategy. Skip ahead to Chapter 11 (Legal Protections) and Chapter 12 (Mental Health) first, and reach out to Fair Work or a legal service immediately.
